Show webcam

 

If the Video Conferencing option is enabled by the meeting site’s administrator, the webcam icon will be enabled. The moderator may select an attendee and open his or her webcam window. Depending on the meeting site’s configuration, a maximum of 12 simultaneous webcam windows can be opened. The attendee has the option to allow or deny the request to open the webcam window. If the attendee does not have a webcam, the video part of the window will not be visible but the microphone part of the window will still function.

 

The webcam window also includes voice over IP. If you are already on an audio conference call, you should click on the microphone icon in the webcam window to mute the microphone so you don’t hear echo. Click on the webcam or microphone settings icon to select the camera or microphone devices attached to your computer.

 

You can resize, minimize, move, and maximize the webcam windows. The moderator can control all attendee’s webcam windows. The webcam windows are automatically minimized when a presentation or screen sharing is started (the option can be disabled by clicking the drop-down menu next to the webcam icon under the Attendees Window in the moderator’s Meeting Viewer.) To restore the window, simply click on the minimized video window.

 

 

You can click on the drop-down menu icon next to the webcam icon to display a list of options for controlling the webcam windows. For instance, you can disable the automatic minimization of webcam windows when a presentation is started.

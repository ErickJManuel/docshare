Meeting Viewer

 

The Meeting Viewer opens in a browser window when a meeting starts and includes the following components.

 

  • Shared Display – The large background picture in the center of the screen is the main sharing area. The default background picture can be changed to another one by adjusting the Meeting Viewer properties prior to the meeting. Anything you share during the meeting, such as a whiteboard, a presentation, or portions of your screen will be viewable to the participants. During a sharing session, participant control the zoom ratio of the Shared Display area or show it in the full screen mode.
  • Meeting Toolbar – The top toolbar allows the moderator or a presenter to control meeting functions including sharing screen, whiteboard, library content, sending screenshots or files. Participants are allowed to set their emoticons, which will be displayed next to their name in the Attendees Window. The moderator may also record a conference if the recording option is enabled.
  • Information Window – The Information Window shows the meeting title, ID, host name and audio conference information.
  • Attendees Window - The Attendee Window lists attendee names, and includes a toolbar for controlling participants' permissions. You can grant drawing or presenter permissions, or bring up the webcam (if the optional is enabled) of the selected attendee. If audio conferencing and conferencing controls are enabled, the list may also show the telephone numbers of the callers and allow the moderator to mute/un-mute a caller, dial-out, hang up a call, and match a caller’s number with an attendee name.
  • Messaging Window – The Messaging Window allows participants to send chat messages to everyone or another participant. In addition, any files or screenshots that are sent will be available for download here.

 

You can resize or hide the above windows by dragging the vertical divider between the windows and the Shared Display area.

 

The Meeting Viewer is embedded in a web browser window. Participants can any time during a meeting refresh the browser to reconnect to a meeting. The meeting session is persistent and participants can resume the same meeting any time.

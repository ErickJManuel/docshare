Terminology

 

  • Member: A member is a user that has an account on the meeting site. A member can be a moderator of a meeting. A member also can be given “Admin” permission to the site.
  • Group: A member belongs to a group. All group members share the same set of hosting services.
  • Meeting Site: A meeting site is where all members sign in to manage their meetings.
  • Meeting Room: A meeting room is a placeholder for all meetings by a member. Each member has exactly one meeting room.
  • Meeting: A member can create multiple meetings. Each meeting has a unique meeting ID and URL and can be held any number of times. A meeting can be scheduled, and/or require registration or password.
  • Meeting URL/ID: Each meeting has a unique URL that allows anyone to join the meeting by typing the URL to a web browser. In addition, a meeting also has a 7-digit ID that can be entered by a participant directly in the meeting site’s home page without entering the URL.
  • Session: Each instance of a meeting is called a session and will appear as a session record in the meeting reports.
  • Meeting Viewer: A meeting viewer is used by all participants to attend a meeting. The meeting viewer is Flash-based and works with most OS and browsers.
  • Presenter Software: Certain presenter functions, such as screen sharing or desktop snapshot, require the use of Presenter software. The presenter will be asked to download Presenter Windows Client or Java Client if he or she invokes those functions.
  • Moderator: A moderator is the user who starts a meeting. The moderator can grant presenter or drawing permissions to an attendee. Only the moderator can start or end a meeting. There is only one moderator in a meeting.
  • Presenter: A presenter is a user that has been given presenter permission by the moderator. A presenter can share screen, presentation, annotate, start a whiteboard, etc. There can be many presenters in a meeting. The moderator is also a presenter.
  • Attendee: An attendee is a participant in a meeting who is neither a presenter nor a moderator. An attendee can be given drawing permissions by a moderator to annotate on a slide or a whiteboard.
  • Public/Private meetings: A meeting can be published to the “Home” page of the meeting site. A public meeting allows any site visitor to view or attend the meeting without an invitation. A private meeting is only accessible by anyone who is given the meeting URL or ID.
  • Registration: The moderator of a meeting may require a participant to register for a meeting first. The registration data can be accessed by the moderator under his or her account prior to the meeting.
